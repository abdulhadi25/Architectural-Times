]]> What to do with your Wanderlust in the prevailing lockdown? This question has haunted the wanderers and nomads. Being stuck at their homes, all courtesans of tourism and explorers have no way to appease their wanderlust. Nonetheless, ‘Stay home, Save Life’, has truly become the most popular as well as poignant phrase of the era. As a consequence, the global tourism industry is plunged into an abyss. The constant assertion of this phrase has intensified the wanderlust of wanderers. As a result, staying at home has become a nightmare for tourists. Due to the lockdown, the tourism industry has witnessed the most fecund spring. The people with itchy feet are frazzled as they are forced to halt in times of blossoms. But we have good news for all people who are crept by this lockdown and it is hard for them to follow the instructions of the government and say temporarily adieu to their hiking boots and uncharted places. Keeping in mind their agitation, we have plumbed down a few ways to roam around the world and to cope with tourists’ pining.

Virtual Tours 

The first and foremost solution to mollify your wanderlust in the prevalent crisis is to pledge to discover things form your home. Here is good news for tourists that they can easily gratify their longing for tourism with the help of virtual tours. Being stuck at home does not mean it is impossible to cope with the traveling obsession. As an adherent of the digital world, we have exciting opportunities in the form of virtual tours. Now it is credible to have an expedition of many places without leaving homes. Amongst these places, the Vatican, the Guggenheim in Bilbao, and the Natural History Museum in London are leading in providing online tours to their art centers.

Explore and plan through Reading

If you have a habit of reading, the best thing you can do is read books about tourism. Reading and approaching good books about tourism will facilitate you to explore alien and uncharted places form home. The lockdown has offered you the best chances to collect information about unexplored places and tourism. You can also make a list of the uncharted places so after the termination of this lockdown; you can visit them without wasting any further time in planning.

Watch Documentaries

In case you are not a bookworm, we have another adequate way to calm down your obsession with traveling. As we are living in the digital world, we can get the benefit of it in this hard time. The digital world provides us a vast range of sources to quench our wanderlust. Amongst, access to factual films, tourism-based documentaries, and factual sites about tourism are worthy to mention. This way you can even visualize the places and make prints in your mind.

In addition to this, the national tourism organization of Scotland has produced a short documentary of memories of tourists’ visits to Scotland. Featuring stunning visuals from Skara Brae in Orkney to Loch Ken in Dumfries and Galloway, from Airlie Monument in Angus to Dunnottar Castle in Aberdeen shire and many others, it is an emblem of hope.

Pen down about past adventures of traveling

Another interesting thing you can do to entertain your craving is to write about your past adventures. All tourists must have an extensive record of untold stories from their travels and exploration of peculiar places.  Penning down your past adventures and reviving your memories would be as exciting as embarking on a new journey. This stimulating and exciting act will surely satisfy your wanderlust without leaving home. You can also add a twist in it by sharing it with your friends through social media. It’s going to be a great experience of roaming different places without even leaving your home. The physical journey is forbidden but we are not restricted to embark on a journey into the retreats of our minds and revive some of our exciting hunts and share them with the world.
