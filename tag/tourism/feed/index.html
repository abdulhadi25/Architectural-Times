]]> Ranikot fort – A hidden architectural Gem of Pakistan.

Tourism has been given a lot of importance in the past two decades by developing states all around the globe. One of the countries that were least expected to be a desirable tourist location was Pakistan, due to its non-secure image in the global world. However, the country has worked hard to improve its image and had been ranked as the number 1 travel destination by the United States-based luxury and Lifestyle publication. Pakistan covers different landscapes and has various weathers that one may experience during the travel through the country.

Pakistan has gained most of its popularity due to the northern regions of Gilgit Baltistan and KPK province. As these are the famous areas of Pakistan which the locals know about and the international tourism community is aware of. However, many other places carry historical importance and are very significant in terms of architecture. The region/land known as Pakistan has seen a lot of different leaders of the regions who were of different ethnicity before the freedom. So, the architectural sites of Pakistan can be a point of attraction for tourists and the people of interest.

The Great Wall of Sindh:

Most of the population of the world is aware of the Great Wall of China due to its features and history but there are very few who know about the Great Wall of Sindh in Pakistan. The Great Wall of Sindh is the outer boundary of a fort which is known as Ranikot Fort. The length of the fort is more than 31 Km’s and it was declared as a world heritage site by UNESCO in 1993. One may be amazed to know that Ranikot fort holds the title for the largest fort in the world as well. The fort ramparts are usually compared to that of the Great Wall of China.

Ranikot Fort is about 90 Km’s from the city of Hyderabad and about 2 hours of drive from the city Karachi. It lies in Jamshoro district Pakistan and it is situated in the second largest national park of Pakistan (Kirthar National Park). The fort is a marvel for the people who have an interest in history and architecture. One side of the fort is covered by rocky hills and the other three sides are covered by tall walls, the walls of the fort are interspersed with bastions and we can the towers on three different spots are semicircular. The most interesting factor of this fort is that this fort has another small fort at the heart which is called the Miri Fort. It is about 3 Km’s inside from the main gate. The small fort is said to be the residence of the Mir Royal Family.

The structure of the entire fort is made with stone and lime mortar. The walls of the fort are not in a straight line, rather they are built-in zig zag with 4 gates for entry into the fort. Two gates were built parallel to each other, they are built in such a manner that the Sann River crosses diagonally both the gates. The river s along with the skirt of the gates making it difficult to be breached. The other two gates have multi-leveled doors that have floral ornamentation and carved stones on the walls. The main gate is well preserved and can be climbed to get a great view on both sides of the fort. The fort is said to be constructed in 1812 for 1.2 Million Rupees at that time in history. It is a very huge project that was completed at that time. It was the last capital of the Amirs of Sindh. The initial base of the fort had been laid down by Sindh Archeologists but there were renovations made in the late 18th century by the Talpur Mirs.

Why Visit Ranikot Fort:

The fort carried great importance in the history of the region and is the largest for the world, one should visit to see that how structures were constructed over such a huge land area back in the times. The carving of the stone has been recognized by different organizations among the best carvings of the world from historical times. The location of the fort is something that makes it more attractive as there is barren land on one side of the fort and we see an entire river flowing on the other side.

The architecture of the structure is unique and special in its way, with multi-layered doors, the semi-circular towers, and a fort within a fort. The reason for its construction is still unknown but it must have been home to a huge population and the architects who constructed this fort must have been very skilled.

]]> Summer holidays can get frustrating when you have bored kids at home and no place to take them. This is when the entertainment capital of the world can help you out. Dubai is a fantastic city that is not just fun for adults but for kids as well. Explore the glorious city with your entire family and visit these kid-friendly locations today.

Dolphin Bay

Take your kids to the Atlantis on The Palm where they can swim around with one of the friendliest mammals in the world, dolphins. Swimmers of all skills and ages are allowed to interact with natures purest creatures. This is one of the most sophisticated dolphin habitats in the entire planet. The place does not just provide you with a fantastic time, but it also teaches your kids how important it is to preserve the natural habitats of the dolphins.

Wild Wadi Water Park

The Wild Wadi Water Park is another fantastic theme park that revolves around Arabian Folklore. From 10 in the morning to 6 in the evening, your kids will definitely go crazy. This amazing waterpark offers many of the wildest and exhilarating rides in Dubai and a great place to spend your Sunday. The best thing about the Wild Wadi Water Park is that it caters to the needs of all ages, for example, the water park has a massive pool dedicated primarily to smaller kids. You and your little ones can have a blast surfing all day long. The place is actually considered one of the best water parks in Dubai.

Dubai Fountain

Beautiful lights, illustrious waterworks, and blasting music is the magical combo that you sure don’t want to miss. The Dubai Fountain captivates its audience with an amazing showcase of glistening water moving up and down to the beat of the music. This is actually one of the best places to take your kids at night in Dubai. Trust me; the little ones will brag about it to their friends for months on end. The Dubai Fountain consists of the world’s largest performing fountain that shoots water up to impressive heights. Hypnotizing the viewers with its full continuum of color, the fountain performs to a variety of different songs from contemporary to classical Arabic and world music. Do keep in mind that the fountain operates from 6 to 11.30 pm.

Bollywood Park

Who doesn’t like a bit of a glimpse and glamour? Another fun place you can take your kids in Dubai is the fantastic Bollywood Park. It is actually one of the largest theme parks in the world that is solely dedicated to Bollywood. Your kid would get to experience the Bollywood style of emotion, music, comedy adventure, action, flavor, and literally everything that the Bollywood film industry is made of. You and your family can have fun watching various live entertainment pieces, stage performances, and cinematic rides. The entire park is spread across about 1.7 million square feet. The Bollywood Park is only open on Saturdays and Sundays from 2 to 11 pm.

KidZania

KidZania is an entertainment-packed, fun-filled place designed to provide an adult experience scaled down in size. It’s situated inside the Dubai Mall and offers kids the opportunity to play grownups, doing jobs such as chefs, firemen, radio show hosts, and even doctors. KidZania is a place where the kids can act as Emirate pilots and even produce their own signature scents. It really doesn’t get any better than that! The place also has a Space Center that mimics the real international space station where the kids get to learn about real-life astronauts. At KidZania, the kids get to have loads of fun and as a bonus learns about different professions.

Dreamland Aqua Park

This is another amazing water park that is fun for the whole family. Waterparks are always a great place to have fun with kids, and this one is no different. The Dreamland Aqua Park is an exclusive place where adventurous rides are abundant, which includes rides like ‘Family Raft Rides’ and ‘Twisters.’ The first allows the whole family to take part in, and the other one allows the rider to flow through different twists before splashing into a massive pool. It’s a great place to hang out with your kids on a hot summer day. The waterpark also offers a large open Jacuzzi where you can relax and a dead wave pool for you to unwind. The park is open from 10 to 6 pm. So grab your bathing suits and head over to the Dreamland Aqua Park and splash around with your kids.

VR Park

With the fast progress of technology, who doesn’t love a good old fashioned video game? Mind you, the VR Park in Dubai offers much more than simple arcade games like Pac Man. The VR Park at Dubai Mall is an amazing mix of augmented and virtual reality with a stunning effect. You and your kids can try the adrenaline pumping Burj Drop experience, where you are propelled down the side of the tallest hotel in the world, the Burj Khalifa. The VR Park doesn’t just cater to kids but to teens as well. You can navigate a spaceship to safety or kill zombies at the Walking Dead Outbreak VR game. This is a place that you should definitely not miss, and it’s great for all ages. So head down to the Dubai Mall and take in the VR experience. Trust me; it’s going to be really difficult to take your kids home after a trip to the VR Park.

Dubai Aquarium and Underwater Zoo

Marine animals, they really are a sight for sore eyes. Dubai gives you an opportunity to explore the exotic marine life at the Dubai Aquarium and Underwater Zoo. The aquarium actually holds the record for the World’s Largest Acrylic Panel. Spend the day surrounded by stingrays, jellyfish, giant crocodiles, sharks, and many more marine mammals and fish. The aquarium consists of a 48-meter walk through a tunnel that allows viewers to walk and take a look at animals through a glass partition. The Dubai Aquarium is one of the largest aquariums in the world.

Dubai is like a fun gold mine, and you should definitely not miss out on all the excitement. Above are just some of the places to visit in Dubai with kids, and there are a billion more. So pack your bags and head out to Dubai today.
