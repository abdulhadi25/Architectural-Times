]]> Pakistan National Council of Arts and National Art Gallery in Islamabad, is Pakistan’s first national art gallery. In a nationwide design contest in 1989, the design of Suhail Abbasi & Naeem Pasha of M/s Suhail & Pasha, Architects & Planning Consultants Islamabad was selected for the construction of the NAG ( National Art Gallery).

The gallery, covering an area of 1800 square yards has 14 galleries with adjacent display areas, lecture halls, workshop and storage facilities, laboratories and a library. The four-storey gallery has modern facilities, including an auditorium with capacity for 400, a theatre, stage facilities and an open-air theatre.

While celebrating a month of Japanese culture, The embassy of Japan in collaboration with the Islamabad Art Festival 2019 presented a joint music performance of Pakistani and Japanese artists titled “ NIHON Unplugged: cultural music performance”  at auditorium of Pakistan national council of arts on 24th November,2019 which was sponsored by Pakistan japan friendship forum.

While welcoming the Japanese artists to Pakistan,  Mr. Junya Matsuura, Deputy Chief of Mission, Embassy of Japan in Pakistan, his opening remarks said that  the Embassy of Japan in Pakistan has always struggled to share Japanese culture with Pakistani people as we believe that sharing each other’s culture brings people together. He said Japan and Pakistan share a friendly bond, which has strengthened over the years.

The Japanese percussionist Mr. Takanabe together with a saxophone player Ms. Tabata  and pianist  Ms. Kitagawa mesmerized the audience and won a great applause at auditorium of Pakistan national council of arts
