When we talk about architecture, the architecture of London is the most predominant specimen. London is a city where we can find the world’s renowned and striking architecture styles ranging from the old to new, traditional to modern, art deco to gothic, and many trends in between. Nonetheless, London is one of the most entrancing cities with reference to architecture. The architecture of London is something that brings the lost essence of traveling and enables one to peep into the time of its architecture. In point of fact, more than 100 decades of history can be witnessed at London’s magnificent House of Parliament, elongating fantastically around the corners of Westminster. However, London’s skyline is varying remarkably with a new trend of iconic buildings. Notably, the captivating architecture of London has been receiving massive attention over the last decade. It would not be wrong to say that London has an endless encounter with angles and emptiness. The skirmish of those angles and emptiness is productive in the variety of fascinating and incredible buildings which amplifies the charm of the city. Verily, London has an extensive assortment of architectural styles from a multiplicity of historical periods. Its great architectural scope is a predominant outcome of its long history, persistent redevelopment, and destruction caused by The Great Fire of London and The Blitz. Thence, the city of London is replete with the conspicuous and incredible buildings without any distinction of its type. There are an equal variance and charm in the historic and modern architecture. In point of fact, London is the embodiment of the world’s most striking and mesmeric architecture. The paper dealt with the elucidation of a few spell-binding buildings which are the hallmarks of London city.

Amongst, the most prominent ones is the Gherkin which was designed by the renowned architect Norman Foster in 2003, situated in the heart of the city. It is considered as one of the most alluring modern skyscrapers. The exemplary architecture of London; Gherkin is fundamentally stretched out, hooked, shaft with a rounded end that is redolent of a stretched egg.

In the context of its majesty, the Tower Bridge of London has a leading position. As we all know, London is considered a home to a number of iconic bridges that connect the north with the south. Each of them is designed in its own appealing and swaying way than the former. Tower Bridge, with all its charms, connects Southwark with Tower.

In the same regard as iconic beauty, the National Theatre is worthy to mention. The National Theater is an epochal building on the South Bank and is one of London’s notable edifices. Here is another essential and majestic project of London is the Shard that another of London’s most eminent skyscrapers. It was designed by a legendary Italian architect Renzo Piano. Evidently, it is the most soaring building in the UK and the largest skyscraper in the European Union. These specimens of spectacular beauty and endeavor have set London apart from other European capitals.
