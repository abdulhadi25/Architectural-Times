5 BEST PLACES TO VISIT IN PAKISTAN

July 20, 2020

Pakistan comes in the list of those countries that have the earliest civilizations of the world. Pakistan has some of the tremendous landmarks and historical places that portray the true values of our culture and history. Pakistan is a country eminent for its rich cultural history and diversity. Pakistan is a country that is absolutely an eye candy for tourists and the main reason is that it has got a taste for all types of tourists around the world. Pakistan has beautiful mountains, landscapes, large cities with amazing sites, and historic places with alluring architectural buildings. The best places to visit in Pakistan are the ones that are quite famous because of its rich history and also because one of the oldest civilizations of the world had lived where Pakistan is standing today.

The most famous and best places to visit in Pakistan:

Here is the List of Most famous places in Pakistan you can visit with your friends or family.

Rohtas Fort, Jehlum:

This magnificent Fort is located near Jhelum. Rohtas Fort is also known as Qila Rohtas. Rohtas Fort is a timeworn fort which consists of about twelve gates in total, Sohail Gate, Kabuli Gate, Shishi Gate, Shah Chandwali Gate, Shishi Gate, Tulla Mori Gate, Gatali Gate, Talaqi Gate, Pipawala Gate, Langar Khani Gate, Khas Khani Gate, and Sar Gate. Inside this fort, there are other things such as a museum, mosque, shrine, Phansi Ghat, Rani Mahal, and langar khana gates. There are three water wells in the fort as well. Rohtas Fort is said to be one of the largest forts in the subcontinent. The architecture of this place is the combination of Hindu and Afghan architecture.  The walls of Rohtas fort are high and strong and are made with limestone and sandstone.

 Mohenjo Daro, Sindh:

Mohenjo-Daro is perhaps the most enticing historic place in Pakistan that is also world-renowned. It is located in Larkana District, Sindh. It is at the bank of the mighty stream Indus, which makes it even more breathtaking. The archeological remains of the place take us back to the antiquity of 3000 BC, making it one of the earliest major cities of the world. These beautiful ruins are about 5000 years old. The fact that this place is so antique, makes it even more amazing.

Wazir Khan Mosque, Lahore:

The Walled City of Lahore is a historical section of the city Lahore which used to be guarded by walls all around and had 13 gates. In today’s time, the walls are not there but most of the gates are still standing. The Wazir Khan Mosque in Old Lahore, named after the governor who constructed it in 1634, can be reached by passing through one of the gates called the Delhi Gate. This grandiose mosque has four minarets and five domes. One of the most beautiful mosques in Pakistan, it is best known for its incredible mosaics made of glistening colored glazed tiles on all the external and internal walls, and are so precise and vivid in details which makes Wazir Khan mosque an amazing piece of art.

Lahore Fort, Lahore:

Lahore Fort is an ancient building located in old Lahore. It is said to be built in the second half of the 16th century. Its original structure was razed and rebuilt with burnt bricks and since then this fort was modified several times. These modifications epitomize Pakistan’s astounding cultural heritage through different artistic influences seen all over in its extensive range of different buildings such as mosques, mausoleums, palaces, audience halls, etc. Lahore Fort’s journey tells us a lot about the history of Pakistan which is the main reason why it is the major attraction for tourists.

Khaplu Palace, Gilgit:

Khaplu Palace also known as Yabgo Khar is an ancient fort and palace which is located in Khaplu, Gilgit. The palace is considered an architectural heritage and a huge tourist attraction. It was built in the mid-19th century. Khaplu Palace was built with the assistance of Kashmiri and Balti craftsmen. The structure of the palace has Tibetan, Kashmiri, Ladakhi, Balti, and Central Asian influences.  Yabgo palace has four floors built with timber wood, mud bricks, and clay and soil mortar. It has a carved wooden gate that Raja Hatim Khan took from a fort in Skardu. The palace has a wooden ceiling which is crafted with designs using paints and chisel. The top floor of Khaplu palace has a hall that was used as a leisure room, from which Karakoram mountain range and lawns around the palace can be seen. There are other prominent rooms in the palace including the Royal meeting, Royal balcony, Princess dressing room, and Queen room.

These are a few out of many amazing places of Pakistan that can be visited for sight-seeing. Tourists from all over the world come and discover beautiful historical places in Pakistan. These historical places of Pakistan are beautifully breathtaking. These historical places of Pakistan are an amazing attraction for any traveler or sightseer.
